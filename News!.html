If I were queen for a day . . . by Caitlin Moran

It's not all pomp and ceremony when our columnist becomes monarch. Though there are still a lot of crowns. And more unicorn horns than you might expect


At some point – when waiting in a supermarket checkout queue, or in a traffic jam, or while cleaning out the bin-juice from the bottom of a bin with a hose while wearing your slippers, miscalculating the velocity of the water, getting oomska-riddled bin-juice all over your legs, and feeling very aggrieved about the suboptimal way that life has turned out – we've all thought it. “What would it be like if I were queen for the day? What would that be like? Would it be better than this?”

“I'm just putting in a call about a corgi,” she said, “and you'd do yourself a favour by practising doing a tiny wave while looking a bit peeved – as if you've just seen the handles on your so-called "bag for life" snap after you've done a shop at the big Tesco. You know, the Queen's typical face. GO!”
